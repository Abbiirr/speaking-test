# IELTS Speaking Question Bank (Practice)

> This is a large **practice** question bank compiled from common IELTS Speaking themes and the official test format. It is not an official list of live exam questions.

## Sources used for topic coverage and format

- British Council free IELTS Speaking practice tests (format + sample Part 1/2/3 question styles).
- IDP IELTS guidance on common Part 1 topics and example question types.

## Part 1 — Introduction & Interview (Topic banks)

Use these as a rotating bank. In the real test you typically get 2–3 topics, ~4–5 minutes total.

### 1. Work / Study

1. Do you work or are you a student?
2. What do you like most about your work/studies?
3. What is the most challenging part of your work/studies?
4. If you could change one thing about your work/studies, what would it be?

### 2. Home / Hometown

1. Where are you from?
2. What do you like about your home area?
3. How has your hometown changed in recent years?
4. Would you recommend your hometown to visitors? Why/why not?

### 3. Accommodation

1. What kind of place do you live in?
2. How long have you lived there?
3. What do you like about your accommodation?
4. What kind of place would you like to live in in the future?

### 4. Daily routine

1. What is a typical weekday like for you?
2. Which part of the day do you feel most productive?
3. Do you prefer mornings or evenings? Why?
4. Has your routine changed in the last few years?

### 5. Free time

1. Do you have much free time these days?
2. How do you usually spend your free time?
3. Do you prefer spending free time alone or with others? Why?
4. Is there a new hobby you would like to try?

### 6. Hobbies

1. What hobbies do you enjoy?
2. How did you become interested in that hobby?
3. How often do you do it?
4. Do you think hobbies are important? Why/why not?

### 7. Friends

1. How often do you meet your friends?
2. What do you usually do together?
3. Do you prefer a small group of close friends or many friends? Why?
4. Has friendship changed for you as you got older?

### 8. Family

1. Who do you spend the most time with in your family?
2. What do you like doing with your family?
3. Are you similar to your parents in any way?
4. How important is family to people in your country?

### 9. Food

1. What kind of food do you like?
2. Do you prefer eating at home or eating out? Why?
3. Is there a traditional dish from your country you recommend?
4. Have your eating habits changed over time?

### 10. Cooking

1. Do you like cooking?
2. How often do you cook?
3. What was the last thing you cooked?
4. Do you think children should learn to cook? Why/why not?

### 11. Shopping

1. Do you enjoy shopping?
2. What kinds of things do you usually buy?
3. Do you prefer online shopping or shopping in stores? Why?
4. Has your shopping style changed in recent years?

### 12. Clothes

1. What kind of clothes do you usually wear?
2. How important is fashion to you?
3. Do you wear different clothes for different occasions?
4. Do people in your country care about brands?

### 13. Weather

1. What is the weather like where you live?
2. What kind of weather do you enjoy most? Why?
3. Does weather affect your mood or plans?
4. How do people in your country usually deal with extreme weather?

### 14. Seasons

1. Do you have a favourite season?
2. What do you usually do in that season?
3. Is there any season you dislike? Why?
4. Do seasons affect the way people live in your country?

### 15. Transport

1. How do you usually travel around your city?
2. What is public transport like where you live?
3. Do you prefer driving or using public transport? Why?
4. How could transport be improved in your area?

### 16. Travel

1. Do you like travelling?
2. What kind of places do you enjoy visiting?
3. Do you prefer travelling alone or with others? Why?
4. What place would you like to visit in the future?

### 17. Holidays

1. Do you prefer short breaks or long holidays?
2. What do you usually do on holiday?
3. Do you like planning holidays in detail? Why/why not?
4. What makes a holiday memorable for you?

### 18. Technology

1. Do you use technology a lot in your daily life?
2. What piece of technology do you use most often?
3. Is there any new technology you want to try?
4. Do you think people rely too much on technology?

### 19. Mobile phones

1. How often do you use your phone each day?
2. What do you mainly use your phone for?
3. Do you prefer texting or calling? Why?
4. Do you think children should have smartphones?

### 20. Social media

1. Do you use social media?
2. What do you like and dislike about social media?
3. How has social media changed communication?
4. Do you think social media affects mental health?

### 21. Internet

1. How important is the internet in your life?
2. What do you do online most often?
3. Do you think people have enough privacy online?
4. What would be difficult without the internet?

### 22. Books / Reading

1. Do you enjoy reading?
2. What kinds of books do you like?
3. Do you prefer paper books or e-books? Why?
4. Did you read a lot as a child?

### 23. Music

1. What kind of music do you like?
2. When do you usually listen to music?
3. Do you prefer live music or recorded music? Why?
4. Has your taste in music changed?

### 24. Films

1. What kind of films do you like?
2. Do you prefer watching films at home or at the cinema? Why?
3. How often do you watch films?
4. Is there a film you watched recently that you enjoyed?

### 25. TV programmes

2. What kinds of programmes do you enjoy?
3. Do you prefer watching alone or with others?
4. Has streaming changed the way you watch TV?

### 26. Sports / Exercise

1. Do you do any exercise?
2. What kinds of sports are popular in your country?
3. Do you prefer exercising indoors or outdoors? Why?
4. What motivates people to stay fit?

### 27. Health

1. What do you do to stay healthy?
2. Do you think people today are healthier than in the past?
3. How important is sleep for you?
4. What habits would you like to improve?

### 28. Art

1. Do you like art?
2. Have you ever visited an art gallery?
3. What kind of art is popular in your country?
4. Do you think art is important in education?

### 29. Photography

1. Do you like taking photos?
2. What do you usually take photos of?
3. Do you prefer using a phone or a camera? Why?
4. How do photos help people remember things?

### 30. Nature

1. Do you like spending time in nature?
2. What natural places are near where you live?
3. How often do you go to parks or outdoor places?
4. What do you think people can do to protect nature?

### 31. Animals

1. Do you like animals?
2. Did you have a pet when you were a child?
3. What animals are common in your country?
4. Do you think people should keep pets? Why/why not?

### 32. Plants / Flowers

1. Do you have a favourite plant or flower?
2. Do you keep any plants at home?
3. Are flowers commonly given as gifts in your country?
4. Do you think greenery is important in cities?

### 33. Water

1. Do you drink a lot of water every day?
2. Do you prefer bottled water or filtered water? Why?
3. Is water quality a concern where you live?
4. What can people do to save water?

### 34. Time management

1. Are you good at managing your time?
2. What do you do when you are busy?
3. Do you prefer making plans or being spontaneous?
4. What wastes people's time most these days?

### 35. Learning English

1. When did you start learning English?
2. What do you find most difficult about English?
3. How do you practise English outside class/work?
4. Why do you want to improve your English?

### 36. Neighbourhood

1. What is your neighbourhood like?
2. Do you know your neighbours well?
3. What facilities are near your home?
4. Is it a good area for families? Why/why not?

### 37. Celebrations

1. Do you like celebrating birthdays?
2. What is an important festival in your country?
3. How do people usually celebrate special occasions?
4. Do you prefer small or big celebrations? Why?

### 38. Gifts

1. Do you like giving gifts?
2. What kinds of gifts are common in your country?
3. Do you prefer practical gifts or sentimental gifts? Why?
4. Have online shopping and delivery changed gift-giving?

### 39. Public places

1. What public places do you often go to?
2. Do you like visiting cafés or restaurants?
3. Do you prefer quiet places or lively places? Why?
4. What public place would you improve in your city?

### 40. Languages

1. How many languages can you speak?
2. Do you think it is important to learn foreign languages?
3. What is the best way to learn a language?
4. Do you think translation apps will replace language learning?

## Part 3 — Discussion (Abstract questions linked to Part 2)

Use these to practise extending ideas: reasons, examples, comparisons, and future predictions.

### Theme 1: Work and productivity

1. How do working patterns differ between younger and older people in your country?
2. What makes a job satisfying besides salary?
3. Do you think working from home will become more common? Why/why not?
4. How can companies improve employees’ work-life balance?
5. Should employers provide training for all staff? Why?

### Theme 2: Education

1. What skills should schools focus on besides exam results?
2. How has education changed in your country in the last 20 years?
3. Should university education be free? Why/why not?
4. How can teachers motivate students who are not interested?
5. Do you think online learning can replace classrooms?

### Theme 3: Technology and society

1. How has technology changed the way people communicate?
2. What are the disadvantages of relying on smartphones?
3. Do you think children should learn coding at school?
4. How can people protect their privacy online?
5. Will AI create more jobs or replace jobs overall?

### Theme 4: Media and information

1. How do people in your country usually get news?
2. Why do some news stories spread faster than others?
3. Should social media platforms be responsible for misinformation?
4. Do you think people trust online reviews? Why/why not?
5. How can individuals judge whether information is reliable?

### Theme 5: Consumer behaviour

1. Why do people buy things they do not really need?
2. How has online shopping changed local businesses?
3. Are customers more likely to complain nowadays? Why?
4. How important is good customer service for a company’s success?
5. Should companies always accept returns and refunds? Why/why not?

### Theme 6: Environment

1. What environmental problems are most serious in your country?
2. How can individuals reduce waste in daily life?
3. Should governments punish people who pollute? Why/why not?
4. Do you think renewable energy will fully replace fossil fuels?
5. How can cities be designed to be greener?

### Theme 7: Health and lifestyle

1. Why do some people find it hard to maintain healthy habits?
2. Do you think mental health is taken seriously in your country?
3. How has modern life affected sleep and stress?
4. Should governments do more to promote public health?
5. Is fitness more of a trend now than in the past?

### Theme 8: Family and relationships

1. How have family roles changed compared to the past?
2. What do you think makes a strong friendship?
3. Should parents plan children’s leisure time? Why/why not?
4. Do you think people are marrying later these days? Why?
5. How does technology affect family communication?

### Theme 9: Culture and traditions

1. Why do traditions matter to societies?
2. How do celebrations differ across generations?
3. Should schools teach traditional culture? Why/why not?
4. How does globalisation affect local culture?
5. Do you think traditional festivals will change in the future?

### Theme 10: Cities and housing

1. Why do many people prefer living in cities?
2. What problems do large cities face most?
3. How can governments make housing more affordable?
4. Do you think tall buildings are a good solution to housing shortages?
5. How important are public spaces in cities?

### Theme 11: Transport and travel

1. How can public transport be improved in your country?
2. Why do some people prefer cars even in crowded cities?
3. Do you think people travel more now than in the past? Why?
4. How does tourism affect local communities?
5. Should governments limit car use in city centres?

### Theme 12: Science and research

1. How interested are most people in science in your country?
2. What scientific discovery has had the biggest impact on daily life?
3. Who should fund scientific research: governments or companies?
4. Do you agree that there are still major discoveries left to make?
5. How can the public learn about scientific developments?

### Theme 13: Art and creativity

1. Why is creativity important in modern jobs?
2. Should governments fund the arts? Why/why not?
3. How do films and music influence young people?
4. Is it easier to become an artist today than in the past?
5. Do you think art can change society?

### Theme 14: Sport and leisure

1. Why do some people dislike exercise?
2. How can cities encourage people to be more active?
3. Do you think professional sports are too commercialised?
4. Should schools require daily physical education?
5. What are the benefits of leisure activities for older people?

### Theme 15: Crime and rules

1. Why do people break rules even when they know the consequences?
2. How can communities prevent crime besides policing?
3. Do you think punishments are too strict or too lenient today?
4. Should CCTV cameras be used widely in public places? Why/why not?
5. How can young people be encouraged to follow laws?

## How to use this bank (fast routine)

1. Pick **2 Part 1 topics** (answer 4 questions each, record yourself).
2. Pick **1 cue card** (1 minute notes + 2 minutes speaking).
3. Pick the matching **Part 3 theme** (answer 3–5 questions).
4. Review: note repeated words, grammar slips, and pauses.
