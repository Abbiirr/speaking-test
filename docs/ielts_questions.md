# IELTS Speaking Questions (from provided images)

## Test A — Flowers and plants / Reviews / Customer service (Test 3, p.54)

### Part 1 — Flowers and plants
1. Do you have a favourite flower or plant? **[Why/Why not?]**
2. What kinds of flowers and plants grow near your home? **[Why/Why not?]**
3. Is it important to you to have flowers and plants in your home? **[Why/Why not?]**
4. Have you ever bought flowers for someone else? **[Why/Why not?]**

### Part 2 — Cue card
**Describe a review you read about a product or service.**

You should say:
- what you read the review about  
- what information the review gave about the product or service  
- and explain what you did as a result of reading this review  

### Part 3 — Discussion topics

#### Online reviews
1. What kinds of things do people write online reviews about in your country?
2. Why do some people write online reviews?
3. Do you think that online reviews are good for both shoppers and companies?

#### Customer service
1. What do you think it might be like to work in a customer service job?
2. Do you agree that customers are more likely to complain nowadays?
3. How important is it for companies to take all customer complaints seriously?

## Test B — Jewellery / Science TV programme / Science and the public (p.95)

### Part 1 — Jewellery
1. How often do you wear jewellery? **[Why/Why not?]**
2. What type of jewellery do you like best? **[Why/Why not?]**
3. When do people like to give jewellery in your country? **[Why?]**
4. Have you ever given jewellery to someone as a gift? **[Why/Why not?]**

### Part 2 — Cue card
**Describe an interesting TV programme you watched about a science topic.**

You should say:
- what science topic this TV programme was about  
- when you saw this TV programme  
- what you learnt from this TV programme about a science topic  
- and explain why you found this TV programme interesting  

### Part 3 — Discussion topics

#### Science and the public
1. How interested are most people in your country in science?
2. Why do you think children today might be better at science than their parents?
3. How do you suggest the public can learn more about scientific developments?

#### Scientific discoveries
1. What do you think are the most important scientific discoveries in the last 100 years?
2. Do you agree or disagree that there are no more major scientific discoveries left to make?
3. Who should pay for scientific research — governments or private companies?
